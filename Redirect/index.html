SDGs
NO POVERTY
GOAL
Despite progress, global poverty remains daunting, with 736 million people surviving on less than $1.90 a day in 2015, lacking essentials like food and clean water. Challenges endure, particularly for women and in regions like South Asia and sub-Saharan Africa. Climate change and conflict worsen poverty, underscoring the SDGs' urgency to eradicate poverty by 2030 through focused interventions and aid for vulnerable communities.
SDGs
NO POVERTY
GOAL TARGET
  • By 2030, at least half of all forms of poverty must be eliminated.
  • Put in place social safety nets for everyone.
  • By 2030, ensure that the impoverished and vulnerable are adequately covered.
  • Make sure everyone has equal access to financial resources and essential services.
  • Bolster the impoverished and vulnerable's resistance to climate-related disasters.
  • Organize resources, such as increased collaboration on development.
  • Create frameworks for policies that support the impoverished at the national, regional, and global levels.
